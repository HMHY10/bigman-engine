# UK Competitor Pricing — Organic Serums on Amazon
**Research date:** 21 April 2026
**Scope:** Top 5 competitors selling organic face serums on Amazon UK, ranked by review volume and bestseller presence.

---

## Methodology

Amazon UK product pages returned CAPTCHA blocks during direct scraping. Data was compiled from:
- Amazon UK search results and product metadata
- Price comparison sites (Idealo, PriceRunner, Bargainfox)
- Third-party retailer listings (BigGreenSmile, MyPure, AllBeauty)
- Review aggregators (ReviewIndex, ReviewMeta)
- Curated ranking sites (FindThisBest UK)

⚠️ REVIEW NEEDED — Prices should be verified directly on Amazon UK before commercial use. Prices correct to best available data as of April 2026.

## Key Findings

### Pricing landscape
- **Budget tier (£8–£10):** Eclat Skincare and Dr Organic compete at the accessible end. Eclat dominates on volume; Dr Organic leads on credentials.
- **Mid tier (£10–£15):** Garnier and PurOrganica offer larger volumes at competitive £/ml rates. Garnier carries mainstream brand trust; PurOrganica competes on bottle size.
- **Premium tier (£15–£20):** UpCircle positions as ethical/premium with a strong sustainability narrative. Higher £/ml justified by B Corp status and brand story.

### Review landscape
- Eclat Skincare's 30,000+ reviews are exceptional and distort the category — this is likely built partly through an Amazon-first strategy over several years.
- Certified organic brands (Dr Organic, UpCircle, Garnier) have significantly fewer reviews but stronger credentials.
- PurOrganica's ~1,000 reviews at £13 suggests reasonable traction in the mid-market.

### Positioning gaps
- **High-review + certified organic** is an unoccupied sweet spot — UpCircle has the credentials but not yet the review volume.
- Products above £15/30ml with robust COSMOS certification and strong review counts are absent from this top 5.
- The 100ml+ format at premium-organic pricing is underserved (Satin Naturel at £14.95/100ml is the closest but is Germany-based).

### Threats to watch
- **Garnier Organic range** may be partially discontinued — if confirmed, this removes a key mid-market competitor.
- **Eclat Skincare's** review solicitation history could be exploited if a formal complaint is lodged with Amazon or the ASA.
