## Key Market Observations

### Weleda dominates the certified organic segment
Weleda appears twice in the top 10 and consistently holds the highest ratings (4.5–4.6★). Their COSMOS Certified Natural positioning combined with aggressive promotional pricing (25% off RRP) makes them the benchmark competitor. They appear to be running sustained promotional discounts.

### Price clustering at the budget end
Most organic serums are priced between £10–£16 for 30ml. Consumers in this category appear price-sensitive. Products above £20 (Argan Oil serum at £24.46) need a strong differentiation story to justify the premium.

### Review volume is relatively low across the category
Even the top performers have modest review counts (110–382). This indicates the genuine organic serum sub-category is still a smaller, more specialist niche compared to mainstream serums (Garnier Vitamin C has 24,229 reviews at £10.17 in the same search).

### Third-party marketplace risk
La Saponaria and the Argan Oil serum lack Amazon featured offers. This significantly reduces conversion rates and makes pricing less stable. Any ArryBarry listing should aim to secure the featured offer/Buy Box.

### COSMOS certification is a differentiator
Only Weleda and SKOHO hold COSMOS certification among visible competitors. This is a meaningful trust signal for health-conscious consumers and underused in the market.
